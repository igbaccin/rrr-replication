## Critiques

**Common mechanisms/themes:**
- Through the adherence to traditional hierarchical order within communities, women's economic and social spheres are influenced (Bryceson_2002 p.9)
- As a result of diminished labor time and economic returns from cash cropping, men's role as family provisioners is undermined (Bryceson_2002 p.9)
- With the removal of farmer subsidies in major grain-producing countries, international staple food prices rise, driving dependence on non-agricultural economic activities (Bryceson_2002 p.11)
- Through the growing division of labor and the rise of workshop production, which eventually led to the emergence of the factory system (Humphries_2013 p.18)
- By having a lower burden of dependency due to lower French fertility compared to Britain (Humphries_2013 p.19)
- Through the prosperity of reactionary parties uninterested in economic growth (as seen in France after the Unfinished Revolution of 1789), institutions hindered long-run economic growth.
- By conservative reasons, institutions throttled economic growth in Spain, despite its hegemonic status in the sixteenth century.
- The summed wills of suppliers and demanders, as understood by economists, can influence economic growth through the notion familiar to them.
