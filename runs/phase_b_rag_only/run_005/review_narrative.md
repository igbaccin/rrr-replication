## Supports

**Common mechanisms/themes:**
- Through the persistence of certain institutions from the past, economic performance is affected
- By reducing expropriation risk or improving other aspects of institutions, significant gains in income per capita are achieved
- The specific cluster of institutions has a large effect on long-run economic growth
- Through the elaboration of mixed economies as a function of economic growth
- By encouraging and sustaining capital investment and effective demand under macroeconomic and monetary conditions
- Via the development of mammoth diversified conglomerate corporations
- Through the organization and distribution of economic prosperity (Acemoglu et al., 2002 p.6)
- By reversing the impact of institutions on long-run development (Acemoglu et al., 2002 p.5)

## Critiques

**Common mechanisms/themes:**
- Through the adherence to traditional hierarchical order within communities, women's economic and social spheres are influenced (Bryceson_2002 p.9)
- As a result of diminished labor time and economic returns from cash cropping, men's role as family provisioners is undermined (Bryceson_2002 p.9)
- With the removal of farmer subsidies in major grain-producing countries, international staple food prices rise, driving dependence on non-agricultural economic activities (Bryceson_2002 p.11)
- Through technological advancements in the coal industry during the Industrial Revolution era, the response to increases in demand was facilitated (Clark&Jacks_2007 p.4)
- The extraction cost of coal was influenced by the pithead price and rents paid to landowners (Clark&Jacks_2007 p.14)
- Through the prosperity of reactionary parties uninterested in economic growth (as seen in France after the Unfinished Revolution of 1789)
- By conservative reasons that throttled economic growth in Spain despite its hegemonic status in the sixteenth century
- Via the nearby and spectacular examples of bourgeois economic and political successes in Holland, England, Scotland, and America counterfactually not present in France

## Complicates

**Common mechanisms/themes:**
- Through the non-inclusion of home production in statistical data, the contribution of African women to economic growth and development has been historically undervalued.
- The colonial economy's confinement of the majority of African women to home production and reproductive functions has hindered their access to education and economic opportunities.
- Through industrialization in Japan and Europe, income disparities were caused in the early twentieth century.
- By buying a wide range of non-food items, the rapid growth of the German economy led to rising real wages for workers in Leipzig.
- Through the wages and prices of building craftsmen, economic development is influenced (Allen_2001 p.4)
- Capitalist economic development benefits the working class after 1815 (Allen_2001 p.23)
- Through the reinforcement of trade between Africa and Western Europe (and parts of Asia), institutions played a key role in shaping long-term economic growth.
- Institutional change, particularly in Europe, was a significant factor driving the rise of Europe and its long-term economic growth.
