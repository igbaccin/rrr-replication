## Critiques

**Common mechanisms/themes:**
- Through the groundswell of livelihood diversification not being recorded by large-scale surveys (Bryceson_2002 p.2)
- By men's labor time and economic returns from cash cropping diminishing, undermining their role as family provisioners (Bryceson_2002 p.9)
- As a result of rising international staple food prices due to the removal of farmer subsidies in major grain-producing countries (Bryceson_2002 p.11)
- Through the growing division of labor and the rise of workshop production, which eventually led to the emergence of the factory system (Humphries_2013 p.18)
- By having a lower burden of dependency due to lower French fertility compared to British fertility (Humphries_2013 p.19)
- Through the prosperity of reactionary parties uninterested in economic growth (as seen in France after the Unfinished Revolution of 1789)
- Through conservative reasons that throttled economic growth in Spain despite its hegemonic status in the sixteenth century
- Through industrial development, educational expansion, and military autonomy, institutions contribute to long-run economic growth (Simson_2019 p.27)
