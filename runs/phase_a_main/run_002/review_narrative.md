## Critiques

**Common mechanisms/themes:**
- Through the growing division of labor and the rise of workshop production, which eventually led to the emergence of the factory system (Humphries_2013 p.18)
- By having a lower burden of dependency due to lower French fertility compared to British fertility (Humphries_2013 p.19)
- Through the prosperity of reactionary parties uninterested in economic growth (as seen in France after the Unfinished Revolution of 1789), institutions hindered long-run economic growth.
- By conservative reasons, institutions throttled economic growth in Spain, despite its hegemonic status in the sixteenth century.
- The summed wills of suppliers and demanders, as understood by economists, can influence economic growth through the notion familiar to them.
- Through the economic factors that drive changes in environmental impacts over the course of economic development
- By assuming that environmental damage does not reduce economic activity sufficiently to stop the growth process and that any irreversibility is not so severe that it reduces the level of income in the future
- Through the availability of sources, determined by patterns of consumption (vanZanden_1999 p.6)

## Complicates

**Common mechanisms/themes:**
- Through the non-inclusion of home production in statistical data, the contribution of African women to economic growth and development has been undermined
- Historically, gender roles have influenced social rankings, with male metal workers being ranked higher than female potters
- Through industrialization in Japan and Europe, income disparities were caused in the early twentieth century.
- By buying a wide range of non-food items, the rapid growth of the German economy led to rising real wages for workers in Leipzig.
- Through the cheaper construction and operation of railways compared to lorries, institutions facilitated economic growth.
- By bringing the total population in line with the revealed rate of growth between 1931 and 1960, institutional changes accelerated population growth.
- The commercial exploitation of natural resources located in the south and labor migration from the north, enabled by institutions, contributed to economic growth.
- Through the reinforcement of trade and institutional change, economic growth was stimulated (Austin_2008 p.26)
