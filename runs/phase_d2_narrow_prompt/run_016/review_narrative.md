## Supports

**Common mechanisms/themes:**
- Urbanization and income are positively related through the proliferation of cities in preindustrial Europe (AcemogluEtAl_2002 p.10)
- The productive countryside and flourishing trade network support the existence of cities, thereby contributing to economic prosperity (AcemogluEtAl_2002 p.10)
- Through high productivity in the tradable sector, wages of north-western Europe were higher
- The increase in rice price led to a sharp decline in grain wages
- A rise in English grain wage and a decline in Indian grain wage caused divergence in unskilled silver wages
- Through political rent-seeking and offering benefits to rulers, guilds secured market privileges.
- By coercively intervening in serf transactions, overlords exerted comparative advantage over craft guilds in shaping pre-industrial monetary policy.
- The preference for guns in the capture and transport of slaves is taken as evidence of their advantage (Whatley_2018 p.4)
