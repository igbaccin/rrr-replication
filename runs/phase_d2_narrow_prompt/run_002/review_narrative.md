## Supports

**Common mechanisms/themes:**
- Through high productivity in the tradable sector, north-western Europe had high silver wages
- High agricultural productivity led to low prices of grain in southern India
- The gap between 'silver wage' and 'grain wage' indicates the level of development
- Through political rent-seeking and offering benefits to rulers, guilds secured market privileges.
- The comparative advantage of overlords in providing protection to serfs allowed for coercive intervention in serf transactions.
- Through the measure of excess capacity in the British gunpowder industry, the craft guild regulation may have influenced pre-industrial monetary policy transmission
- The growth of slave exports along the Guinea Coast of Africa in the 18th century could be explained by the immediate impact of gunpowder on slaves (21%)
- Through the sudden and spectacular decline in the skill premium after the Black Death
