## Supports

- Through the forces of institutional change acting to limit growth in Western countries

- As countries readjust the balance in favor of productivity growth
- Through the persistence of certain institutions that are detrimental to investment and economic progress (AcemogluEtAl_2001 p.8)
- By the long-term preservation of early institutions, which impacted economic performance (AcemogluEtAl_2001 p.27)
- By exploiting differences in institutions as a source of exogenous variation to estimate their impact on economic performance (AcemogluEtAl_2001 p.27)
- Through the design of institutions by politically powerful groups to maximize rents for European colonists rather than long-run growth (AcemogluEtAl_2002 p.33)
