## Critiques

**Common mechanisms/themes:**
- Through the time bind, economic thought is limited to short-term perspectives (Braudel&Wallerstein_2009 p.14)
- By focusing on earlier periods, historians search for ancient economies that may influence long-run growth (Braudel&Wallerstein_2009 p.14)
- The shift towards economic and social history over political history may impact the understanding of long-term growth conditions (Braudel&Wallerstein_2009 p.7)
- Through the shift in importance from economic base to superstructure as economic conditions deteriorate (Hopkins_2019 p.9)
- By relocating the emphasis on economic forces within African societies (Hopkins_2019 p.8)
- As global financial crises bring economic forces to the forefront of world order discussions (Hopkins_2019 p.10)
- Through access to employer gifts and cottage gardens, the range and sufficiency of diet may impact long-run economic growth (Humphries_2013 p.4)
- The lack of monotonic rise in real wages or living standards throughout the eighteenth century could potentially hinder long-term economic growth (Humphries_2013 p.3)
