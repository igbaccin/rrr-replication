## Supports

**Common mechanisms/themes:**
- Through the forces of institutional change acting on Western countries
- By the elaboration of the mixed economy being a function of economic growth itself
- The persistence of certain early institutions (such as constraints on government expropriation, independent judiciary, property rights enforcement, and equal access to education) over time leads to detrimental effects on investment and economic progress.
- Economic mechanisms that lead to institutional persistence for a long time contribute to the continuity of detrimental institutions.
- Through the design of institutions by politically powerful groups to maximize rents for European colonists (not long-run growth)
- By an institutional reversal resulting from European colonialism
- Because equilibrium institutions may be extractive, even though they do not encourage economic development
- Through the economic provision of husbands, the contribution of African women to economic growth and development is largely dependent (Akyeampong&Fofack_2014 p.9)
